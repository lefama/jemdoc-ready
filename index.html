Bio

Leandro F. Maia is an incoming assistant professor in the School of Mechanical, Industrial, and Manufacturing Engineering at Oregon State University. He received his Ph.D. in Industrial and Systems Engineering from Texas A&M University, where he was advised by Dr. David H. Gutman. He also holds a Master’s degree in Mathematics from UFPA and a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Engineering from the Military Institute of Engineering (IME), Brazil.

Research Interest

Lately, I’ve been particularly interested in the development and analysis of algorithms for large-scale optimization. My current focus includes:

  • Augmented Lagrangian methods.

  • Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) and its variants for large-scale and distributed optimization.

  • Trust Region methods, especially in the context of nonsmooth and inexact proximals.

  • Randomized Block Coordinate Descent (RBCD) techniques for decomposable problems.
